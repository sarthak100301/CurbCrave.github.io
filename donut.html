............and the donut just stood there with a glazed expression.

What a beautiful story it was! It’s not easy to find a love tale which so much purity in expression and truth these days.
Oh, donuts! you guys just missed a beautiful storytelling session.
But don't you worry, because there is no hurry, we would gladly repeat this scrumptious story.
This is a tale of true love and unrequited desire, that blossomed between our health conscience champion, Mr. Cooper and the good old Mr. Donut. But the refined flour and it’s shooting glycemic index refrained the two from professing their undying feelings and getting a happily ever after. Saint Valentine was disheartened and sent CurbCrave onto a mission with one goal and one goal in mind and that was to conclude the story by catalyzing the unsaid love confessions and complete the relationship.
CurbCrave put on its Ethan Hunt gear and kicked the refined flour out of the equation. But then how were the donuts made, you ask? We will tell you.
Half cup (125 ml) milk was borrowed from Mrs. Moo and dry yeast was added to it along with 1 tsp sugar. This concoction was left for 10 mins at room temperature.
In another bowl, 2 tbsp olive oil and 4 tbsp sugar were mixed to which 1 cup of whole wheat flour, 1 tsp baking powder, ½ tsp baking soda and a pinch of salt was added.
The activated yeast was added last and a soft dough was made which was covered with a damp cloth (fact: for activation of gluten).
This dough was rested at room temperature for 2 hours.
Using a special rolling pin borrowed from an authentic Indian kitchen, the dough was rolled flat at about half an inch thick and then donuts and donut holes were cut out (Jake Perlata’s favourite). These were again left to rest for 15-20 minutes.
And they were fried Oh no! Our hero, CurbCrave didn’t stop here. The special instrument called Air-Fryer was set to pre-heat at 165° for 7 minutes and the donuts were baked after for 8 minutes.
A glaze by mixing 1 tbsp icing sugar, 1 tbsp milk and 1 tsp vanilla essence was made and hot donuts bathed in it for 10 seconds.
They were then cooled down and our health conscience champion, Mr. Cooper sighed in the anticipation of contentment that he was about to bask in.
After being cooled down, it was placed on a pretty plate (because why not!) and the donut just stood there with a glazed expression.
